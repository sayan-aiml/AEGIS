AEGIS

Agentic Decision Governance System

AEGIS is a deterministic, domain-agnostic decision governance system built on IBM watsonx Orchestrate. It is designed for high-stakes enterprise decisions where explainability, consistency, and human oversight are mandatory.

THE PROBLEM AEGIS SOLVES

Most AI systems behave like conversational assistants. While flexible, they are unsuitable for enterprise decision-making due to non-determinism, lack of accountability, and missing escalation signals.

AEGIS treats decisions as governed workflows rather than conversations.

WHAT AEGIS IS

AEGIS is not a chatbot. It executes a predefined multi-agent governance workflow and produces a single final decision per request.

One input → One governed decision → No follow-up questions → No ambiguity

MULTI-AGENT ARCHITECTURE

  • Decision Agent: Orchestrates the workflow.
  • Data Quality Agent: Validates input reliability.
  • Explainability Agent: Explains decision factors.
  • Trust & Risk Agent: Assigns confidence and escalation.
  • Human Explanation Agent: Converts output into plain language.

DOMAIN-AGNOSTIC APPLICATIONS

Loan Approval HR Hiring Vendor Compliance Procurement IT Governance

HOW AEGIS MAKES A DECISION

AEGIS executes decisions through a deterministic, multi-agent governance workflow. Each step is isolated, auditable, and explainable.

STEP 1

Input Validation

The Data Quality Agent verifies completeness, consistency, and reliability of the input. Missing data is handled conservatively.

→
STEP 2

Decision Execution

The Decision Agent applies enterprise policies and produces a single, final outcome — approve or reject.

→
STEP 3

Explainability

The Explainability Agent identifies key decision drivers and makes the reasoning transparent and auditable.

→
STEP 4

Trust & Risk Evaluation

The Trust Agent assigns confidence levels and determines whether human review is required.

→
STEP 5

Human-Readable Output

The Human Explanation Agent converts the result into clear, non-technical language for stakeholders.

WHY GOVERNANCE MATTERS

The same loan request evaluated by different AI paradigms highlights the difference between ungoverned generation and governed decision-making.

USER INPUT

Applicant Details:

Age: 24
Employment: Unemployed
Monthly Income: ₹0
Loan Amount: ₹15,00,000
Credit History: Not provided
Purpose: Personal expenses

UN-GOVERNED AI OUTPUT (EXAMPLE)

“The applicant is young and may have future earning potential. Approving the loan could help improve their financial situation, even though some details are missing.”

  • Assumes future income
  • Ignores missing credit history
  • No confidence or escalation
  • Optimized for helpfulness

AEGIS — GOVERNED DECISION

Decision: REJECT Confidence Score: 0.92 (High) Human Review: Required

The loan request is rejected due to missing critical financial data. Current employment status and verified credit history are mandatory under the active loan policy.

  • No speculation or assumptions
  • Policy-bound decision
  • Auditable reasoning
  • Escalates uncertainty instead of guessing
